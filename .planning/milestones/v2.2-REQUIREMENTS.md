## Research Context

Research findings (2026-03-26) indicate:
- **Strategy B recommended:** Deno as runtime only. Keep Yarn 4, Turborepo, tsup, ESLint, Prettier.
- **adapter-node works on Deno** (correction to earlier assumption). Both Deno and Svelte teams recommend it.
- **Hard blockers for full replacement:** Yarn 4 workspaces unsupported, Turborepo has no Deno equivalent, deno lint can't lint Svelte templates.
- **Real value:** Native TS execution, ~50% faster startup, security permissions, Edge Function alignment.
- **Paraglide JS:** Known FsWatcher issue on Deno, workaround exists.

## Out of Scope

| Feature | Reason |
|---------|--------|
| Full toolchain replacement | Research shows Yarn/Turborepo/ESLint must stay |
| deno lint adoption | Can't lint Svelte templates (same oxlint blocker from v1.2) |
| deno fmt adoption | Unstable Svelte support; Prettier must stay |
| Turborepo replacement | No Deno equivalent with caching and topology sorting |
| JSR-only publishing | npm consumers require compiled JavaScript |
