Section 3 — Why path-b sub-option (b2) preserves these 5 tests by construction
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Plan 86.1-01 maneuver (sub-option b2 per RESEARCH §3.4 verdict) modifies
ONLY `tests/tests/fixtures/voter.fixture.ts` (the post-Likert Skip-Next tail's
iteration cap). It does NOT call `applyLikertOnlyFilter()`, does NOT modify
`packages/dev-seed/src/templates/e2e.ts`, and does NOT alter the default
`data.setup.ts` seed pipeline.

Consequence: the seed shape consumed by the `voter-app` Playwright project
remains identical to its pre-fix state. The sort-17 categorical question
(`test-question-directional-1`), the sort-18 boolean, and the sort-19+ number
are ALL still seeded into the database. The 5 PASS_LOCKED tests above continue
to find their assertion target in the DOM exactly as before.

This is the load-bearing safety property of sub-option (b2):
  - Path (a) — in-place `data.setup.ts` filter edit — would REGRESS these 5
    tests (RESEARCH §3.3 verdict).
  - Path (b1) — clone setup + split project — solves the regression risk but
    has medium project-topology blast radius (must rewire `voter-app`'s
    `dependencies: ['data-setup']` and risks conflicting with the 5 directional-
    asserting tests that live in the SAME `voter-app` project).
  - Path (b2) — fixture-helper bump — contains the fix to one file (voter.fixture.ts)
    AND does not mutate seed shape. The 5 PASS_LOCKED dependencies are
    preserved by construction.

D-09 (no PASS_LOCKED regression) satisfied a priori.
