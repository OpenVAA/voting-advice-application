### 3.4 Conflict resolution: project split or test-level override

The naive path (b) doesn't work because the 6 fixture-fragile tests AND the 5 directional-asserting tests share the same `voter-app` Playwright project. Three sub-options for Plan 86.1-01:

**Sub-option (b1) — Split `voter-app` into two projects.** Create `voter-app-likert-only` (depends on `data-setup-likert-only`) for fixture-fragile tests and keep `voter-app` (depends on `data-setup`) for the directional + filter-matrix tests. **Sub-option (b2) — Keep one `voter-app` project on the default seed; override `answeredVoterPage` fixture to bypass the broken Likert-loop tail.** Modify `voter.fixture.ts` to detect non-Likert opinion questions in the loop and Skip-Next past them (uses the existing Phase 77 P02 3-iteration tail at lines 95-102 but raises the cap or makes it walk past arbitrary non-Likert types). **Risk:** medium (fixture-helper changes affect all consumers; needs per-plan smoke). **Lower-leverage** — only fixes voter-app cells, not the variant-constituency cascade-tail.

**Research recommendation: sub-option (b2) is the lowest-risk path.** It treats the fixture-fragility as the root cause (it is) and contains the fix to one fixture file (`voter.fixture.ts`). **Planner decision point:** sub-option (b1) vs (b2) is a judgement call. (b1) is the more orthodox structural fix (mirrors Phase 85-03's variant-multi-election approach but for the default `voter-app`); (b2) is the smaller-blast-radius incremental fix.
