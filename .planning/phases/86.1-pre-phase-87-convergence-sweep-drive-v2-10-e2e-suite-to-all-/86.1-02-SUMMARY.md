## Hypothesis History (per RESEARCH §5.1-§5.5)

### H1 — Modal close-race / dialog-close settle pattern (Phase 86-02 baseline)
**Verified-applied, EMPIRICALLY INSUFFICIENT.** Phase 86-02 commit `5d67f1933` replaced `toBeHidden()` with `toHaveCount(0)` on the dialog-wrapper locator `getByRole('dialog').filter({ has: getByTestId('feedback-form') })`. Mechanism: `toBeHidden()` had stale `aria-hidden` evaluation during close. Status as of Plan 86.1-02 start: code in place + verified by 85-04 trace-restored run; assertion STILL fails. Spec lines 75-86 + 100-105 carried the H1 block-comment.

### H2 — Multi-dialog locator collision per Pitfall 8 (PLAUSIBLE, unexplored)
RESEARCH §5.2 favored H2 as the next hypothesis: a second `<dialog role="dialog">` (feedback popup, entity-details drawer, or other) may be open simultaneously, causing the `.filter({ has: feedback-form })` chain to match an unintended element. The 85-04 trace cleanup removed the `test.use({ trace: 'off' })` from this spec — full trace artifacts are now produced on failure. Plan 86.1-02 did NOT inspect a failing trace (the H4 mitigation was attempted first per RESEARCH §5.4's "form-element direct assertion bypasses any dialog-wrapper accessibility-tree staleness during the close transition" recommendation). H2 remains plausible; surfaced as recommended next action #1 in the v2.11+ todo.

### H3 — Svelte 5 reset semantics (UNLIKELY per RESEARCH §5.3)
The failure is on the `toHaveCount(0)` close-assertion (upstream of any `reset()` semantics), not on the `toHaveValue('persistence test text')` reopen assertion. H3 was deprioritized in Plan 86.1-02 scope per RESEARCH §5.3.

### H4 — Close-transition selector window (Plan 86.1-02 fix-attempt — EMPIRICALLY DISPROVED)
**Mechanism:** Playwright's `.filter({ has: getByTestId('feedback-form') })` inspects the DOM (not the a11y tree), so the dialog-wrapper lingers during close-transition. **Mitigation:** replace `expect(feedbackDialog).toHaveCount(0)` with `expect(page.getByTestId('feedback-form')).toHaveCount(0)` — directly assert the form element's absence rather than the dialog wrapper's role-tree state. **Applied:** Task 1 commit `937ec5147` at lines 86 + 105. **Empirical result:** per-spec smoke shows the form-element assertion ALSO fails with `9 × locator resolved to 1 element` (the smoke capture is in `post-fix/86.1-02-smoke-feedback-persistence.txt`). **Disproof:** Feedback.svelte is kept mounted via `bind:this={feedbackRef}` in `FeedbackModal.svelte:62` so the form element persists across open/close cycles — this is BY DESIGN per spec docstring lines 87-91 ("Feedback is kept mounted via bind:this in FeedbackModal:62"). The persistence-across-cancel assertion REQUIRES the form element to survive. H4 is logically incompatible with the test contract.

### Conclusion
Both H1 and H4 are wrong. The true close signal is a different DOM mechanism — most likely the `<dialog open>` attribute on the wrapper, or `aria-hidden`/`inert` attributes that ModalContainer applies during close. v2.11+ should capture a trace artifact frame-by-frame to identify the actual mechanism, then redesign the close-signal contract (see todo recommendations §1-§4).
